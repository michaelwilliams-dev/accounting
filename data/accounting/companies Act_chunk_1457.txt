The period specified under subsection (1)(c)— must begin with the date on which the notice of proposed penalty is given to the body, and must not be less than 21 days. Written representations made by the body before the end of the period specified under subsection (1)(c) must be considered by the Secretary of State. After considering any such representations or, in their absence, on the expiry of the period specified under subsection (1)(c), the Secretary of State must decide— whether to impose a penalty, and where the Secretary of State decides to do so, whether to reduce the proposed amount of the penalty. The Secretary of State must give notice of the decision (a “penalty decision notice”) to the body. Where the Secretary of State decides to impose a penalty, the penalty decision notice must— state that the Secretary of State has imposed a penalty on the body and its amount, identify the requirement or obligation in question and state— why it appears to the Secretary of State that the requirement is not satisfied or the obligation has not been complied with, or where, by that time, the requirement is satisfied or the obligation has been complied with, why it appeared to the Secretary of State when giving the notice of proposed penalty that the requirement was not satisfied or the obligation had not been complied with, and specify a time by which the penalty is required to be paid.
