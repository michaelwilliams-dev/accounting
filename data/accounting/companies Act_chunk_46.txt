Power to direct change of name in case of company ceasing to be entitled to exemption If it appears to the Secretary of State that a company whose name does not include “limited” or any of the permitted alternatives— has ceased to be entitled to exemption under section 60(1)(a) or (b), or in the case of a company within section 61 or 62 (which impose conditions as to the objects and articles of the company)— has carried on any business other than the promotion of any of the objects mentioned in subsection (3) of section 61 or, as the case may be, subsection (2) of section 62, or has acted inconsistently with the provision required by subsection (4) or (b) of section 61 or, as the case may be, subsection (3)(a) or of section 62, the Secretary of State may direct the company to change its name so that it ends with “limited” or one of the permitted alternatives. The direction must be in writing and must specify the period within which the company is to change its name. [F46(2A) The period must be a period of at least 28 days beginning with the date of the direction. (2B) The Secretary of State may by further direction in writing extend the period.
