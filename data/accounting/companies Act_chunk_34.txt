This section applies to a document that is (or purports to be) executed by a company in the name of or on behalf of another person whether or not that person is also a company. Common seal A company may have a common seal, but need not have one. A company which has a common seal shall have its name engraved in legible characters on the seal. If a company fails to comply with subsection (2) an offence is committed by— the company, and every officer of the company who is in default. An officer of a company, or a person acting on behalf of a company, commits an offence if he uses, or authorises the use of, a seal purporting to be a seal of the company on which its name is not engraved as required by subsection (2). A person guilty of an offence under this section is liable on summary conviction to a fine not exceeding level 3 on the standard scale. This section does not form part of the law of Scotland. Execution of deeds A document is validly executed by a company as a deed for the purposes of section 1(2) (b) of the Law of Property (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1989 (c. 34) and for the purposes of the law of Northern Ireland if, and only if— it is duly executed by the company, and it is delivered as a deed. For the purposes of subsection (1)(b) a document is presumed to be delivered upon its being executed, unless a contrary intention is proved.
