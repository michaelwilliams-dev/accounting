For the purposes of this section an “actuarial investigation” means an investigation made into the financial condition of an authorised insurance company in respect of its long-term business— carried out once in every period of twelve months in accordance with rules made under [F1119Part 9A] of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, or carried out in accordance with a requirement imposed under section 166 of that Act, by an actuary appointed as actuary to the company. In this section “long-term business” means business that consists of effecting or carrying out contracts of long-term insurance.
