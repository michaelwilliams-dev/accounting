Guidance Register a limited company as a subcontractor or apply for gross payment status Use Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) form CIS305 to register a company as a subcontractor, apply for gross payment status, or both. From: HM Revenue & Customs (/government/organisations/hm-revenue-customs) Published 1 August 2014 Before you start If you do not want to apply for gross payment status, you can register as a subcontractor with payment under deduction online (http://www.gov.uk/what-you-must- do-as-a-cis-subcontractor/how-to-register). Register online To fill in the online form, you’ll need the company’s: Unique Tax Reference (UTR) current turnover bank account details evidence of turnover — such as bank statements, payment and deduction statements, invoices and contracts You’ll also need information about each director and person with significant control, including their: name address UTR National Insurance Number Depending on your circumstances, you may also need: the company registration number the company’s VAT registration number the company’s PAYE reference number any inherited receipts and their details the company’s prospective turnover details of shareholding companies a tax clearance certificate from the tax authority of your company’s home country You’ll need to sign in with your Government Gateway user ID and password (if you do not have a user ID, you can create one when you first try to sign in). If you are an agent, you will need to use the Government Gateway user ID you use to sign in to your agent services account. If you cannot register online You can find information on completing the postal form in Company registration g uidance notes (https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/66854e2bd9d35187868f43f2/CIS305_Notes.p df) (PDF, 104 KB, 5 pages). Get all of your information together before you start. You will fill this form in online and you cannot save your progress.
