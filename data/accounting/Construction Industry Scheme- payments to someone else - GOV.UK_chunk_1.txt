Guidance Construction Industry Scheme: payments to someone else Find out how subcontractors who operate under the Construction Industry Scheme can nominate someone else to receive payments. From: HM Revenue & Customs (/government/organisations/hm-revenue-customs) Published 21 November 2014 Who can get paid on my behalf If you’re a subcontractor operating under Construction Industry Scheme (https://www.gov.uk/what-is-the-construction-industry-scheme) you can choose someone to get payments for you, for example: a workmate relative friend someone you owe money to This person is known as your ‘nominee’. To arrange payments to them you should speak to your contractor. Verify and pay your nominee If you’re registered to g et payments without deductions (https://www.gov.uk/what-you- must-do-as-a-cis-subcontractor/how-to-g et-g ross-payment-status) - known as gross pay - the contractor will pay your nominee the same way, providing: they’ve been able to verify (https://www.gov.uk/what-you-must-do-as-a-cis- contractor/verifying-and-paying-your-subcontractors) you and your nominee your nominee is also registered to receive gross pay If your nominee isn’t registered to get gross payments they’ll receive payments with deductions made.
