(8.12.2017) by The Insolvency (Miscellaneous Amendments) Regulations 2017 (S.I. 2017/1119), reg. 1(1), Sch. 2 paras. 4, 8) 9B Competition undertakings This section applies if— the [F101Competition and Markets Authority] or a specified regulator thinks that in relation to any person an undertaking which is a company of which he is a director has committed or is committing a breach of competition law, the [F101Competition and Markets Authority] or the specified regulator thinks that the conduct of the person as a director makes him unfit to be concerned in the management of a company, and the person offers to give the [F101Competition and Markets Authority] or the specified regulator (as the case may be) a disqualification undertaking. The [F101Competition and Markets Authority] or the specified regulator (as the case may be) may accept a disqualification undertaking from the person instead of applying for or proceeding with an application for a disqualification order. A disqualification undertaking is an undertaking by a person that for the period specified in the undertaking he will not— be a director of a company; act as receiver of a company’s property; in any way, whether directly or indirectly, be concerned or take part in the promotion, formation or management of a company; act as an insolvency practitioner.
