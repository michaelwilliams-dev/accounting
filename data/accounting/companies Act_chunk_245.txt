The reference in subsection (3)(b) to a conviction is to the final decision in the proceedings. For this purpose— a conviction becomes final— if not appealed against, at the end of the period for bringing an appeal, or if appealed against, at the time when the appeal (or any further appeal) is disposed of; and an appeal is disposed of— if it is determined and the period for bringing any further appeal has ended, or if it is abandoned or otherwise ceases to have effect.
