Accidental failure to give notice of resolution or meeting Where a company gives notice of— a general meeting, or a resolution intended to be moved at a general meeting, any accidental failure to give notice to one or more persons shall be disregarded for the purpose of determining whether notice of the meeting or resolution (as the case may be) is duly given. Except in relation to notice given under— section 304 (notice of meetings required by members), section 305 (notice of meetings called by members), or section 339 (notice of resolutions at AGMs proposed by members), subsection (1) has effect subject to any provision of the company's articles. Members' statements Members' power to require circulation of statements The members of a company may require the company to circulate, to members of the company entitled to receive notice of a general meeting, a statement of not more than 1,000 words with respect to— a matter referred to in a proposed resolution to be dealt with at that meeting, or other business to be dealt with at that meeting.
