Ceasing to be a community interest company and becoming a charity: application to court to cancel resolutions Where special resolutions have been passed with a view to a company ceasing to be a community interest company and becoming a charity, an application to the court for the cancellation of the resolutions may be made— by the holders of not less in the aggregate than 15% in nominal value of the company’s issued share capital or any class of the company’s issued share capital (disregarding any shares held by the company as treasury shares); if the company is not limited by shares, by not less than 15% of its members; or by the holders of not less than 15% of the company’s debentures entitling the holders to object to an alteration of its objects; but not by a person who has consented to or voted in favour of the resolutions.
