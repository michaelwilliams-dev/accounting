IFRS 7 Financial Instruments: Disclosures Prior to its deletion in the 2013/14 cycle, the intention of paragraph 6 of FRS 101 was to highlight, for qualifying entities that are companies, that even though the standard includes exemptions from IFRS 7 and IFRS 13, company law requires certain disclosures in relation to financial instruments measured at fair value. Feedback from stakeholders was received indicating that it was not clear if this paragraph applied to qualifying entities that were not companies, and it was potentially confusing. All entities applying FRS 101 (regardless of whether they are companies or not) may take advantage of any disclosure exemptions permitted in the standard unless the law or other relevant legislation requires otherwise. Consequently, paragraph 6 was deleted from FRS 101 and replaced with a general reminder in paragraph 4A highlighting that financial statements prepared in accordance with FRS 101 are Companies Act accounts, not IAS accounts, and therefore must comply with the requirements of company law and any other relevant legislation. 36A In January 2020, the IASB amended IAS 1 inserting paragraphs 76A and 76B that cover the structure and content of the statement of financial position.
