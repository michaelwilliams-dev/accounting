Private company becoming public Re-registration of private company as public A private company (whether limited or unlimited) may be re-registered as a public company limited by shares if— a special resolution that it should be so re-registered is passed, the conditions specified below are met, and an application for re-registration is delivered to the registrar in accordance with section 94, together with— the other documents required by that section, and a statement of compliance. The conditions are— that the company has a share capital; that the requirements of section 91 are met as regards its share capital; that the requirements of section 92 are met as regards its net assets; if section 93 applies (recent allotment of shares for non-cash consideration), that the requirements of that section are met; and that the company has not previously been re-registered as unlimited. The company must make such changes— in its name, and in its articles, as are necessary in connection with its becoming a public company. If the company is unlimited it must also make such changes in its articles as are necessary in connection with its becoming a company limited by shares.
