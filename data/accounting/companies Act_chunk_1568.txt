(2) A person “controls” a right if, by virtue of any arrangement between that person and others, the right is exercisable only— by that person, in accordance with that person's directions or instructions, or with that person's consent or concurrence. (1) “Arrangement” includes— any scheme, agreement or understanding, whether or not it is legally enforceable, and any convention, custom or practice of any kind. (2) But something does not count as an arrangement unless there is at least some degree of stability about it (whether by its nature or terms, the time it has been in existence or otherwise).
