Company's power to withdraw restrictions notice A company that issues a person with a restrictions notice under paragraph 1 must by notice withdraw the restrictions notice if— it is satisfied that there is a valid reason sufficient to justify the person's failure to comply with the notice served under section 790D [F1843or 790E][F1843, 790DA, 790E or 790EA], the notice served under section 790D [F1844or 790E][F1844, 790DA, 790E or 790EA] is complied with, or it discovers that the rights of a third party in respect of the relevant interest are being unfairly affected by the restrictions notice. Supplementary provision (1) The Secretary of State may by regulations make provision about the procedure to be followed by companies in issuing and withdrawing restrictions notices. The regulations may in particular make provision about— the form and content of warning notices and restrictions notices, and the manner in which they must be given, the factors to be taken into account in deciding what counts as a “valid reason” sufficient to justify a person's failure to comply with a notice under section 790D [F1845or 790E][F1845, 790DA, 790E or 790EA], and the effect of withdrawing a restrictions notice on matters that are pending with respect to the relevant interest when the notice is withdrawn.
