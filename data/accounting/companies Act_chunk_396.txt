(See end of Document for details) View outstanding changes Where and for how long records to be kept: offences If a company fails to comply with any provision of subsections (1) to (3) of section 388 (requirements as to keeping of accounting records), an offence is committed by every officer of the company who is in default. It is a defence for a person charged with such an offence to show that he acted honestly and that in the circumstances in which the company's business was carried on the default was excusable. An officer of a company commits an offence if he— fails to take all reasonable steps for securing compliance by the company with subsection (4) of that section (period for which records to be preserved), or intentionally causes any default by the company under that subsection.
