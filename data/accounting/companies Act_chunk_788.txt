Circumstances in which financial assistance prohibited Assistance for acquisition of shares in public company Where a person is acquiring or proposing to acquire shares in a public company, it is not lawful for that company, or a company that is a subsidiary of that company, to give financial assistance directly or indirectly for the purpose of the acquisition before or at the same time as the acquisition takes place. Subsection (1) does not prohibit a company from giving financial assistance for the acquisition of shares in it or its holding company if— the company's principal purpose in giving the assistance is not to give it for the purpose of any such acquisition, or the giving of the assistance for that purpose is only an incidental part of some larger purpose of the company, and the assistance is given in good faith in the interests of the company.
