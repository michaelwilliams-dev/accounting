That person is referred to in this section as “the main transgressor”. The Secretary of State may make an application to the court for a disqualification order against P under this section if it appears to the Secretary of State that it is expedient in the public interest for such an order to be made. For the purposes of this section, P exercised the requisite amount of influence over the main transgressor if any of the conduct— for which the main transgressor is subject to the order made under section 8, or in relation to which the undertaking was accepted from the main transgressor under section 8(2A), was the result of the main transgressor acting in accordance with P's directions or instructions. But P does not exercise the requisite amount of influence over the main transgressor by reason only that the main transgressor acts on advice given by P in a professional capacity. Under this section the maximum period of disqualification is 15 years.
