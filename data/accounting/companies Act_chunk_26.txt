Money payable by a member to the company under its constitution is a debt due from him to the company. In England and Wales and Northern Ireland it is of the nature of an ordinary contract debt. Notice to registrar where company's constitution altered by enactment This section applies where a company's constitution is altered by an enactment, other than an enactment amending the general law. The company must give notice of the alteration to the registrar, specifying the enactment, not later than 15 days after the enactment comes into force. In the case of a special enactment the notice must be accompanied by a copy of the enactment. If the enactment amends— the company's articles, or a resolution or agreement to which Chapter 3 applies (resolutions and agreements affecting a company's constitution), the notice must be accompanied by a copy of the company's articles, or the resolution or agreement in question, as amended.
