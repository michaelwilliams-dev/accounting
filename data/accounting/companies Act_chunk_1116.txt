Privilege against self-incrimination A statement made by a person in response to— a requirement under section 947(1), or an order made by the court under section 955 to secure compliance with such a requirement, may not be used against him in criminal proceedings in which he is charged with an offence to which this subsection applies. Subsection (1) applies to any offence other than an offence under one of the following provisions (which concern false statements made otherwise than on oath)— section 5 of the Perjury Act 1911 (c. 6); section 44(2) of the Criminal Law (Consolidation) (Scotland) Act 1995 (c. 39); Article 10 of the Perjury (Northern Ireland) Order 1979 (S.I. 1979/1714 (N.I. 19)). Annual reports After the end of each financial year the Panel must publish a report. The report must— set out how the Panel's functions were discharged in the year in question; include the Panel's accounts for that year; mention any matters the Panel considers to be of relevance to the discharge of its functions.
