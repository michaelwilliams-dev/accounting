In determining whether to disclose information to a body in accordance with subsection (5), the prescribed body must have regard to the following considerations— whether the use which the other body is likely to make of the information is sufficiently important to justify making the disclosure; whether the other body has adequate arrangements to prevent the information from being used or further disclosed other than— for the purposes of carrying out the functions mentioned in that subsection, or for other purposes substantially similar to those for which information disclosed to the prescribed body could be used or further disclosed. Nothing in this section authorises the making of a disclosure in contravention of [F39the data protection legislation]. [F40(8) In this section, “the data protection legislation” has the same meaning as in the Data Protection Act 2018 (see section 3 of that Act).] 15E.
