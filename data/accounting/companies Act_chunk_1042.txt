A compromise or arrangement sanctioned by the court is binding— on all creditors or the class of creditors or on the members or class of members (as the case may be), and on the company or, in the case of a company in the course of being wound up, the liquidator and contributories of the company. The court’s order has no effect until a copy of it has been— in the case of an overseas company that is not required to register particulars under section 1046, published in the Gazette, or in any other case, delivered to the registrar. 901G Sanction for compromise or arrangement where one or more classes dissent This section applies if the compromise or arrangement is not agreed by a number representing at least 75% in value of a class of creditors or (as the case may be) of members of the company (“the dissenting class”), present and voting either in person or by proxy at the meeting summoned under section 901C. If conditions A and B are met, the fact that the dissenting class has not agreed the compromise or arrangement does not prevent the court from sanctioning it under section 901F.
