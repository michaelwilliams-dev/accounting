If it appears to the court that the application and the evidence produced by the applicant in support of it do not disclose a prima facie case for granting it, the court— must refuse the application, and may make any consequential order it considers appropriate. If the application is not refused under subsection (3)— the applicant must serve the application on the company, the court— may make an order requiring evidence to be produced by the company, and may adjourn the proceedings on the application to enable the evidence to be obtained, and the company is entitled to take part in the further proceedings on the application. On hearing the application, the court may— grant the application on such terms as it thinks fit, refuse the application, or adjourn the proceedings on the application and make such order as to further procedure as it thinks fit.
