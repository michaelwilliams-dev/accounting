Guidance Construction Industry Scheme for businesses based outside the UK Find out what you need to do if you’re a contractor or subcontractor based outside the UK and do work covered by the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS). From: HM Revenue & Customs (/government/organisations/hm-revenue-customs) Published 21 November 2014 Last updated 17 April 2024 — The Construction Industry Scheme (https://www.gov.uk/what-is-the-construction-industry- scheme/if-your-business-is-based-outside-the-uk) (CIS) covers construction work done in the UK and applies whether you’re based in the UK or abroad. There are different registration processes for sole traders, partnerships and companies. How to register Sole traders and partnerships You’ll need to follow the same CIS rules as UK-based contractors (https://www.gov.uk/what-you-must-do-as-a-cis-contractor/register-with-cis) and subcontractors (https://www.gov.uk/what-you-must-do-as-a-cis-subcontractor/register-with- cis). Limited companies You must register before you start work. You can register for gross payment status. To register as a subcontractor for payment under deduction or gross payment status by post you’ll need: to complete section 2 of the CIS registration form (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-as-a-non-resident-company-for-the- construction-industry-scheme) complete the form CIS305 (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/construction- industry-scheme-company-registration-cis305) your original tax clearance certificate from the tax authorities of your company’s home country a completed form 64-8 (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/tax-agents-and- advisers-authorising-your-agent-64-8) if you have an agent working for you To register as a contractor, you’ll need: to complete section 1 of the CIS registration form (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/register-as-a-non-resident-company-for-the- construction-industry-scheme) your original tax clearance certificate from the tax authorities of your company’s home country a completed form 64-8 (https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/tax-agents-and- advisers-authorising-your-agent-64-8) if you have an agent working for you Send all the information to: PT Operations North East England HM Revenue and Customs BX9 1BX Trade in dealing or developing UK land If your business is dealing or developing land in the UK, the profits are taxed in the UK. You’ll need to tell HMRC and register to pay tax as a company or an individual. Further information is available in the Business Income
