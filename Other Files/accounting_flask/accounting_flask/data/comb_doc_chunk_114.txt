This Staff Education Note is written to highlight key areas of consideration when transitioning to FRS 102 and is not designed to be exhaustive. Impairment requirements of financial assets measured at cost or amortised cost Current UK accounting standards There is a general requirement in company law that current assets must not be stated at more than their net realisable value. For those entities not applying FRS 26 there is little detail in current UK accounting standards on impairment of current financial assets (as opposed to fixed assets and goodwill, which are covered by FRS 11 Impairment of fixed assets and goodwill). FRS 102 FRS 102 is more prescriptive, including an explicit requirement for there to be objective evidence of an impairment, which may lead to some changes in practice. For most entities that are not financial institutions, the main financial assets that will be subject to possible impairment are trade debtors (receivables). Most entities are already likely to be making a ‘bad debt provision’, but they will need to ensure that their processes are consistent with the requirements of FRS 102. Application of FRS 102 will not necessarily result in a significant change in the carrying amount of trade debtors. Extract from FRS 102 At the end of each reporting period, an entity shall assess whether there is objective evidence of impairment of any financial assets that are measured at cost or amortised cost.
